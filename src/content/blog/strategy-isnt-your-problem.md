Let's be honest: most of us spend way too much time hunting for that one god-tier indicator or a secret signal group that promises 90% win rates. The internet is flooded with them, yet most traders are still stuck in the red.

The truth is your strategy is rarely the issue.

Most people already have a system that could work. The breakdown happens in the execution. It's the impulsive entries, closing a winner too early because your heart is racing, or revenge-trading after a bad loss. It's not the market, it's the person behind the keyboard.

## The memory trap

The biggest mistake I see, and I've done this too, is relying on memory. You remember the massive win that felt incredible, and you definitely remember the painful liquidation. But the 200 trades in between? They're a blur.

Without actual data, you're just guessing. You're repeating the same mistakes and blaming market manipulation or bad luck when, really, you just don't understand your own patterns.

## Trading is a business, not a hobby

Professional traders don't hope for a good week. They treat this like a business. That means:

- Recording every single entry and exit.
- Taking notes on your emotional state. Were you bored? Angry? Rushing?
- Reviewing the ugly trades, not just the ones you would post about.

It's tedious at first. But this level of detail is what exposes why you're stuck. You might realize you lose a disproportionate share of your money after 3:00 PM, or that your gut feeling trades are actually what's draining your account.
